³ 1. Introduction  ³
ÀÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄ´     Stereograms are also known as spatial images.
                   ³  The trick behind them is the way of our eyes
                   ³  work : the image that we see forms by merging
                   ³  the image that the left eye sees with that of
                   ³  the right eye sees. Also, you got to know that
                   ³  all this happens inside your head. :-)
                   ³
ÚÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÅÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄ
³ 2. I wanna see ! ³
ÀÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄ´     There are several ways to see those images :
                   ³
                   ³ (1) Try a simple one : draw 2 filled circles on a
                   ³     paper. Then stare at them, until you'll see
                   ³     4 points that will slowly merge into 3 points.
                   ³     Accomplish this by staring BEHIND the paper.
                   ³
                   ³ (2) Look close to the screen (monitor) until the
                   ³     image blurs. Then go away very slowly, till
                   ³     you're 30cm (14 inches) off the screen. By now
                   ³     all you got to do is staring until the stereo-
                   ³     image becomes clear.
                   ³
                   ³ (3) If you got a monitor radiation filter, turn
                   ³     luminosity off, look at your image reflected
                   ³     on the filter, then turn on the luminosity.
                   ³     This is a variation of (2).
                   ³
                   ³     It's advisable to try several times, until
                   ³  you'll see a thing. It's difficult the first time-
                   ³  until you got the skill. The keyword is, though,
                   ³  PATIENCE !
                   ³
